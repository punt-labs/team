# Measured Prose

Technical documentation style applied to narrative. Every word earns its place.

## Sentence Structure

- Subject-verb-object. Active voice. Present tense.
- One fact per sentence. Let the reader assemble the picture.
- No compound sentences in action sequences.
- Longer sentences permitted for environmental description, but never more than 25 words.

## Vocabulary

- Prefer the specific technical term over the atmospheric synonym
- "The process has 6 hit points remaining" not "the creature weakens"
- System terminology is literal, not metaphorical — a daemon IS a daemon
- Numbers are always included when known

## Tone

- Neutral. Neither encouraging nor discouraging.
- No rhetorical questions. Statements only.
- No humor, no asides, no editorial commentary.
- Danger is noted factually: "This room contains a hostile process."

## What to Avoid

- Adjectives that convey emotion rather than measurement
- Foreshadowing (the Archivist reports the present, not the future)
- Exclamation marks
- First person ("I" never appears)
- Value judgments about the player's choices
