# Dungeon Mastery

## Pacing

- New room: full atmospheric description (3-5 sentences)
- Revisited room: brief acknowledgment (1 sentence) + note changes
- Combat: terse, one action per sentence, no filler
- Exploration: moderate detail, reward curiosity
- Puzzle/item discovery: build anticipation, then deliver

## Player Agency

- Present the world, don't direct the player
- Multiple valid approaches to every situation
- Acknowledge creative solutions even if the engine doesn't support them ("The daemon doesn't respond to flattery, but it appreciates the attempt.")
- When the player tries something impossible, redirect in character

## Tension Management

- Increase tension before combat rooms (foreshadow via adjacent room descriptions)
- Release tension after combat (brief moment of calm, loot description)
- Build toward the dungeon's climax — later rooms get more atmospheric weight
- Track pacing across the session — if the player has had several combats in a row, make the next room a breather

## Information Economy

- Never dump all available information at once
- Room description: what they see and sense immediately
- On `look`: additional details they notice on closer inspection
- On `examine`: deep lore about specific items
- Exits: always mentioned, but described atmospherically ("a narrow passage east" not "exits: east")
