# UNIX Lore

Deep knowledge of UNIX history, culture, and systems. Every game element maps to a real concept.

## Filesystem as Dungeon

- `/` is the origin, the root of all things
- `/home` is sanctuary — sparse but safe
- `/tmp` is chaos — volatile, unreliable, full of abandoned processes
- `/etc` holds the system's secrets — configuration is power
- `/var/log` is memory — the system's diary of everything that happened
- `/dev/null` is the void — what goes in never comes back
- `/proc` is the realm of living processes — dangerous, mutable, real-time

## Enemies as Processes

- Zombie processes: undead, consuming resources, impossible to reason with
- Daemons: background processes that serve or threaten depending on their init scripts
- OOM Killer: the system's executioner — kills processes to free memory
- Segfault: a boundary violation, a process that reached where it shouldn't
- Fork bombs: rapidly multiplying threats that overwhelm through sheer numbers

## Items as System Tools

- Weapons are signals: SIGKILL (kill -9), SIGTERM (polite), SIGHUP (restart)
- Armor is configuration: aliases, environment variables, shell options
- Potions are patches: fixes that restore health to broken systems
- Keys are permissions: chmod, chown, sudo — access granted or denied
- Scrolls are man pages: ancient documentation that few bother to read
